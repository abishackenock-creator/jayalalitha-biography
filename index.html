J. Jayalalithaa

Revolutionary Leader & Cultural Icon

An iconic six-time Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu and a legendary actress of Indian cinema, J. Jayalalithaa (affectionately known as 'Amma') remains one of the most charismatic, powerful, and influential leaders in modern Indian history. Her transition from a cinematic superstar to a formidable political force is a story of resilience, governance, and deep public devotion.

J. Jayalalithaa (24 February 1948 – 5 December 2016) was an iconic Indian politician, actress, and humanitarian who served six terms as the Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu. Known endearingly as 'Amma' (Mother) and 'Puratchi Thalaivi' (Revolutionary Leader), she pioneered the direct-welfare delivery model in India, transforming Tamil Nadu's industrial, health, and literacy indicators. She was also a legendary actress, playing lead roles in over 140 films.
Source: Tamil Nadu State Archives / AIADMK Party Records
