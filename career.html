A Cinematic Sensation

The Reigning Queen of South Indian Cinema

Entering the film industry at the tender age of 15 to support her family, J. Jayalalithaa rose with blazing speed to become one of the most successful, critically acclaimed, and highest-paid actresses in Indian film history. She shattered industry norms by introducing modern westernized fashion, complex character styling, and highly expressive choreography.

As a trained classical dancer in Bharatanatyam and Kathak, her poise and coordination on screen was unmatched. She had a rare, multilingual capability—delivering dialogue flawlessly in Tamil, Telugu, and Kannada, which allowed her to rule all three industries simultaneously. Her on-screen pairing with M. G. Ramachandran (MGR) across 28 blockbuster movies is widely regarded as the most successful romantic pairing in Indian cinema, laying the groundwork for their future political alliance.

Legendary Filmography & Hit Movies

A curated showcase of her most influential and record-breaking movies.

Tamil1965

Vennira Aadai

Co-star:Srikanth
Director:C. V. Sridhar

Jayalalithaa's debut Tamil film as a lead actress. She played a young, traumatized widow with a maturity that won rave reviews and established her as a premier dramatic actress overnight.

Key Trivia: She was only 17 years old when she played this complex role.
Tamil1965

Aayirathil Oruvan

Co-star:M. G. Ramachandran (MGR)
Director:B. R. Panthulu

A swashbuckling adventure movie that marked her first collaboration with MGR. Their electric chemistry made it a massive box-office hit, starting an iconic partnership of 28 films.

Key Trivia: The film ran for over 100 days in multiple theaters across Tamil Nadu.
Tamil1969

Deiva Magan

Co-star:Sivaji Ganesan
Director:A. C. Tirulokchandar

A landmark drama exploring father-son relationships. Jayalalithaa gave a stellar performance as a compassionate woman who heals a fractured family.

Key Trivia: The first ever Tamil film to be submitted by India for the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film.
Tamil1969

Adimai Penn

Co-star:M. G. Ramachandran (MGR)
Director:K. Shankar

A blockbuster movie where she played a courageous peasant girl and a captive queen. She sang her first playback song 'Amma Endral Anbu' in this movie, which became a household hit.

Key Trivia: MGR set up a special filming camp in Jaipur, Rajasthan, for this high-budget historical epic.
Tamil1972

Pattikada Pattanama

Co-star:Sivaji Ganesan
Director:P. Madhavan

A cultural clash comedy-drama where she played a westernized, headstrong modern woman who learns to respect traditional values, winning the Filmfare Award for Best Actress.

Key Trivia: The movie won the National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Tamil.
Tamil1973

Suryagandhi

Co-star:Muthuraman
Director:Muktha Srinivasan

A highly progressive film about a working woman who earns more than her husband, causing marital friction. Her realistic depiction of female independence won her second consecutive Filmfare Award.

Key Trivia: She sang two popular songs in this film: 'Oh Meri Dilruba' and 'Naan Endral Adhu Naanum Avalum'.
Telugu1972

Sri Krishna Satya

Co-star:N. T. Rama Rao (NTR)
Director:K. V. Reddy

A mythological masterpiece in Telugu cinema. She delivered flawless classical dances and highly articulate dialogue delivery, earning the Filmfare Award for Best Actress - Telugu.

Key Trivia: She acted opposite legendary actor-politician NTR, with whom she starred in multiple Telugu blockbusters.
