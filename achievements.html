PIONEERING REFORMS

Key Achievements

1992
Social Welfare

Cradle Baby Scheme (Thottil Kuzhandhai Thittam)

A pioneering initiative launched to eradicate female infanticide. Families could anonymously place female newborns in cradles set up at government centers, where the state took full custody and provided for their education and upbringing.

2013
Social Welfare

Amma Canteens (Amma Unavagam)

A highly successful municipal food security project serving highly subsidized, healthy food (like idli for ₹1 and sambar rice for ₹5) to millions of daily laborers and the urban poor, serving as a blueprint for other Indian states.

1992
Governance

All-Women Police Stations

Established Asia's first ever All-Women Police Stations in Tamil Nadu. The stations, staffed entirely by female officers, provided a safe space for women to report crimes, domestic abuse, and dowry harassment.

2001
Development

Mandatory Rainwater Harvesting

Passed a landmark legislation making rainwater harvesting structures mandatory in all public and private buildings across Tamil Nadu. This single policy successfully recharged Chennai's depleting groundwater table.

2011
Development

Free Laptop Distribution Scheme

Distributed free laptops to over 3.2 million government school and college students to bridge the digital divide, empowering rural youth with modern technology skills.

1993
Governance

Empowering Women through 69% Reservation

Successfully pushed for the Ninth Schedule amendment to the Indian Constitution to safeguard Tamil Nadu's 69% reservation policy, protecting educational and job opportunities for backward classes.

PROFESSIONAL ARCHIVE

The Journey

Tamil, Telugu, & Kannada Cinema

Leading Actress

1964 - 1980
  • Acted in 140 films, achieving blockbuster status across multiple languages.
  • Introduced modern costumes and dance styles to Tamil cinema, transforming the industry.
  • Formed the most successful silver-screen pair with M.G. Ramachandran in 28 movies.
  • Starred in the Oscar-nominated movie 'Deiva Magan' (1969).
All India Anna Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (AIADMK)

Propaganda Secretary

1983 - 1989
  • Revitalized the party's grassroots support base through extensive statewide campaigning.
  • Articulated the party's socialist ideology and MGR's vision to rural audiences.
  • Drafted policy documents and directed election campaigns with massive public turnouts.
Parliament of India (Rajya Sabha)

Member of Parliament

1984 - 1989
  • Represented Tamil Nadu's interests in the upper house of Indian Parliament.
  • Admired by leaders like Indira Gandhi for her eloquent speeches on national issues.
  • Served as a key liaison between the state and central governments.
Government of Tamil Nadu

Chief Minister (6 Terms)

1991 - 1996, 2001, 2002 - 2006, 2011 - 2014, 2015 - 2016
  • Pioneered massive social welfare schemes (Cradle Baby, Amma Canteens, Rainwater Harvesting).
  • Set up the first all-women police stations in India.
  • Made Tamil Nadu one of the most industrialized and literate states in India.
  • Championed 69% reservation policy to support backward and marginalized communities.
HISTORICAL SIGNIFICANCE

Legacy & Social Impact

6

Terms as Chief Minister

Governed Tamil Nadu across three decades, establishing herself as a formidable administrator.

140+

Films as Lead Actress

Achieved super-stardom in Tamil, Telugu, and Kannada films between 1961 and 1980.

650+

Canteens Established

Amma Canteens serving nutritious food daily to over 300,000 citizens.

3.2M+

School Laptop Users

Students provided with free computing devices, narrowing the rural digital gap.

Social Welfare Leadership

Developed the most comprehensive direct welfare model in India, which includes baby care kits, free food, free laptops, and subsidized medicines, significantly reducing poverty indicators.

Gender Empowerment

Pioneered girl child protection schemes, and set up all-women police stations and women self-help groups (SHGs), transforming gender participation in rural Tamil Nadu.

Infrastructure & IT Growth

Attracted global automobile and tech companies to Chennai, creating millions of jobs and earning Chennai the nickname 'Detroit of South Asia'.

Literacy & Education

Introduced free textbooks, uniforms, bicycles, and laptops, resulting in Tamil Nadu having one of the highest Higher Education Gross Enrolment Ratios (GER) in India (~49%).
